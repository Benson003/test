Ethical Hacking.

In many moives, hackers are potrayed as badguys in moives or spies but in reality their efforts is important in mantaining security and preventing attacks.

Hackers(pentesters) are the ones who test the security and ensure that are software,databases and networks are not vunrable to attacks. Look at this example:
John builds and deploys a chat app users put in personal data and other important credentials. John could wait for a black-hat hacker to hack his database of users' data and the hacker puts the data for sale on the dark web and also lose the respect of his users and buisness or he could get an ethical hacker to pentest his app and make sure users are safe by attacking the app and advicing John on the best line of action to secure his app.

It is a very intresting job becuse it helps you to think creatively as diffrent systems will require diffrent ways of breaking them.
It can sometimes be a time-consuming job and does require patience because some hacks will take days,months and maybe even years but it is very satisfactory to be able to know that you compromised a system(at least I feel that way).

The Hacking Process

  1. Reconnaissance

    It is the first stage in hacking. This involves diging up any information one can find on the target from anywhere like google,youtube,facebook,instagram anywhere, because this can prove to be valuable to as we go on because it can provide potential passwords or exploting a known vulnerablity in such systems.

  2. Scanning Phase

    This involves scanning the target system to find exploits or vulnerablities. It is at this phase that we start to analyze the system from the outside like looking for open ports or trying to exploit legtimate services like web port 80(HTTP) OR 443(HTTPS).

  3. Gaining Access

    This is when we finally through these exploits we gain user access into the system then from there we can pivot into other systems and connect to other internal systems. As ethical hackers we are not permitted to alter,change or steal any data or important information.

  4. Mantaining Access

    This is a stage where the attacker installs backdoors into the system so that in future attacks th systems can easily be accessed. It is usally setup of a local account or ganing passwords to already exsting superuser accounts or using an exploit tool to create such access.

  5. Clearing Tracks

    As any good hacker knows the most important thing is not to get caught. A hacker is expected to remoove tracks and traces of his attack using various methods. It can involve hiding files in alternate data streams,hiding in pictures,maiuplating and rewrting logs.

  6. Reporting

    This is a stage that black hat hackers don't do but ethical hackers do. It involves documenting the process,tools and methods used in the attack, the type of attack and possible solutions to the problem.
